About Me

I'm a UX/UI Designer, Art Director and Journalist.  
Overall, I'm a fun, creative woman who so happens to really loves polka dots.

  • "Work Hard and Be Nice to People"

    That's the motto I've been living by for the past 3 years.

    Ever since seeing these simple words on a beautiful type-based poster in a co-worker's office, I have felt inspired to make it my motto as I began my transition into a new career in web design and development.

    I fell in love with design in high school while taking Journalism 101. My life suddenly had direction after I realized the combined power of journalism and design. My journey led me to Ball State University's premier journalism program, where I majored in Journalism Graphics, and worked as Design Editor for The Daily News, and an Undergraduate Communications Assistant at the Ball State University Alumni Association.

    Post graduation, I held lead design positions at The Roanoke Times, The Times of Northwest Indiana, The Chicago Tribune, and GateHouse Media Corporate. Currently, I am a do-it-all Art Director at Community Magazine Group, creating and developing multiple magazines, websites and marketing materials.

    Ready to widen my skill set, I was introduced to Bloc.io by a colleague. Bloc, an online-based apprenticeship program, allowed me to develop UX/UI design skills over a 36-week online course while still working at my current job. As a graduate of Bloc, I am excited to start a new chapter in my career.

    I have been fortunate enough to use my creative skills in publications and media – and turn it into a passion. The sky's the limit for this next adventure in UX/UI.

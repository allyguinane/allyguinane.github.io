About Me

I'm a UX/UI Designer, Art Director and Journalist.  
Overall, a fun, creative lady who so happens to really loves polka dots.

  • Designer Seeks Change in Career Path

    Raised in rural Indiana, with big dreams of moving to the 'big city', I fell in love with design in high school while taking Journalism 101. I've always been a creative individual, but in a new role as design editor of the yearbook, I was able to use my creative skills in publications and turn it into a passion.

    After attending Journalism Camp, I was hooked on design and Ball State University. I decided to attend its premier program for Journalism Graphics. I thrived in college, joining the Ball State Daily News as Design Editor, and the Alumni Association as an Undergraduate Communications Assistant.

    Post graduation, I continued my journey as I became an news graphics intern at The Florida Times-Union in Jacksonville, Fl. Soon after, I landed my first news job at The Roanoke Times, in beautiful Roanoke, Virginia. I also made career stops at The Times of Northwest Indiana in Munster, Ind., The Chicago Tribune, and GateHouse Media Corporate. Currently, I am a do-it-all Art Director at Community Magazine Group, creating and developing magazines, websites, and marketing materials. See more here.

    I embrace change, and was curious about migrating my skills to the web. Desiring to learn more and a stroke of luck, I was introduced to Bloc.io by an awesome colleague. Bloc is an online-based apprenticeship program where I learned UX/UI skills over a 36-week online course. Now that I have graduated, I am excited to start a new chapter in my career.

    With a big friendly Midwest heart, I really want to use my design skills to create solutions that will help people everyday.
